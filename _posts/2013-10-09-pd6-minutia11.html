Actual Databases

After a brief summary of yesterday's class, we learned about actual real life databases.

  • Flat File: These are essentially large arrays that are similar to filing cabinets. They are good for small sets of data (ex: login information).
  • Relational: Consists of separate tables that are connected by certain entries (ID#, name, course, etc.). Usually larger than flat file databases, but are easier to get answers to quereies from. They sacrifice space for speed.
  • SQL: Stands for Structured Query Language. These are usually created by companies and sold to businesses, banks, etc. Examples of these companies include:
    • Oracle
    • Sybase
    • IBM
    The downside is that it is expensive to buy and maintain these databases.
  • MySQL and PostgreSQL: Slightly different "freeware" versions of SQL. They are not as good because they don't handle transactions well.
  • SQLite: Allows 80% of the functionality of SQL, but instead with a library instead of a database. Favorable over SQL because of the expensive fees that come with SQL (maintenance, etc.).

Ended class by beginning to work on a database that consisted of student superheroes and their CS grades.
